## Key Personnel

### Sean Yeboah (SF) — THE GUY
| PPG | RPG | APG | FG% | eFG% | TS% | Game Score |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|------|-----|-----------|
| 28.9 | 11.5 | 2.7 | 44.8 | 46.7 | 47.5 | 21.1 |

- Accounts for **41.8% of all team points** and **24.8% of rebounds**
- Volume scorer, not efficient (47.5 TS%) — he takes a LOT of shots
- Does not pass much (2.7 APG) — tends to score over distribute
- **REGRESSION FINDING:** Sean scores ~29 whether they win or lose. He is a constant, not a differentiator. However, **his 3-pointers are the single most significant predictor** of win margin (+7.6 points per 3PM, p=0.00001). When he hits 3s instead of grinding contested 2s, their margin explodes. His **usage rate is negatively correlated** with winning (-0.365 per 1% usage, p=0.016) — when the offense becomes the Sean Show, they lose.
- **Game plan: Let him score 2s. Run him off the 3-point line at all costs. Force the offense through him — the more shots he takes, the worse they do. Do NOT double him if it means leaving Benson open.**

## Weaknesses to Exploit

1. **Benson Kas-Vorsah is the real swing factor** — Not Sean. When Benson scores 13+, they win. When he scores 8 or less, they lose. He is the pressure point.

2. **Free throw shooting is TERRIBLE (44.6% FT)** — Every trip to the line is a wasted possession. The regression confirms: FTM is *negatively* correlated with win margin (-0.557, p=0.017).

3. **Sean's usage kills them** — When Sean takes too many shots, they lose. Force the offense to run through him exclusively and watch the team efficiency collapse.

4. **Guard depth is paper-thin** — Only 1 PG and 1 SG. Full-court press and up-tempo play will exhaust Mabumbo and Kas-Vorsah with no relief.

5. **They foul a lot when losing** — 13.0 fouls per game in losses vs 8.1 in wins. Get them frustrated early and they spiral.

6. **Ball movement dies in losses** — Assists drop from 20.4 to 16.0. Aggressive defense disrupts their passing game and forces isolation play.

## Recommended Game Plan for Longshots

### Defense: The Benson Lockdown

1. **Face-guard Benson Kas-Vorsah** — He is defensive priority #1. Not Sean. The data is unambiguous: Benson's scoring is the strongest predictor of their wins. Assign your best perimeter defender to him. Contest every shot. Make him work for everything.

2. **Let Sean score 2s, not 3s** — Sean's 3-pointers are worth +7.6 points of win margin each. His 2-pointers are not a differentiator. Run him off the 3-point line aggressively. Force him into contested mid-range and post-ups. He'll get his 25-30 points but on lower efficiency.

3. **Force the Sean Show** — Deny Benson, deny Janney's passing lanes, and make Sean carry the entire offense. His usage rate is negatively correlated with winning. The more shots he takes, the worse they do as a team.

4. **Don't throw lazy passes near Benson** — His steals actually hurt the AshKnights (he gambles out of position), but you still don't want live-ball turnovers leading to transition.

### Offense: Attack Their Weaknesses

1. **Push tempo** — Their guard depth can't sustain a fast pace. Run them off the floor.

2. **Attack Kas-Vorsah and Mabumbo** — Draw fouls on their only PG and SG. No replacements available. Get them in foul trouble early.

3. **Get them to the free throw line** — At 44.6% FT, every trip to the line is a negative possession for them. Drive and draw contact. Force their bigs to foul.

4. **Boss Baeta and Desmond Raimmy should attack downhill** — Create transition opportunities and force their guards to defend in space.

### Late Game

- At 44.6% FT, intentional fouling is a legitimate strategy in close games
- If Benson is cold, the game is yours to take — press the advantage
- Do NOT let Sean get comfortable from 3-point range in crunch time

### Mentality: Make It Ugly

- They thrive when ball movement is flowing (20.4 AST in wins)
- Physical, disruptive defense turns them into an iso team
- When they go iso through Sean, they lose
- Fewer AshKnights players scoring = smaller win margins for them. Eliminate the supporting cast.

## Summary

The AshKnights are the league's best team but they have clear, data-proven weaknesses. The regression analysis (R-sq = 0.991) shows that their wins are driven by **Benson Kas-Vorsah's scoring** and **Sean Yeboah's 3-point shooting**, while **high Sean usage**, **free throw attempts**, and **Benson gambling for steals** predict their losses. 

The Longshots have already split with them 1-1. The formula is simple: **lock down Benson, chase Sean off the 3-point line, force Sean to carry the entire load, push tempo to exhaust their thin guard rotation, and send them to the free throw line where they shoot 44.6%.** The target is not to outscore them — it's to **hold them under 55 while scoring 55+.** Defense, not offense, decides this matchup. Execute this plan and the AshKnights are beatable.
